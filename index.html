Summary of last year's CS 1 course

Overall, last year's CS 1 course was extremely challenging but worthwhile as I feel that I have greatly strengthened my knowledge of computer science, as well as became proficient in the ever famous "emacs" operating system that the school uses. I believe that I have mastered concepts, ideologies, but most importantly the ability to problem solve. I cannot stress enought the magnitude of prolems and error messages I encountered last year, but after enough head scratching and pondering, I was able to overcome all of them. There is a feeling so rewarding, so satisfying when you get over an obstacle in your code, when you finally see the correct number output, or when you get that IGIS image to appear. Overcoming those excruciatingly tedious problems with peers elicits a bountiful treasure of incomparable joy. Furthermore, the single greatest thing I had the pleasure to experience in last year's CS 1 course was progress on my ISP. The ISP was possibly one of the greatest ways to combine problem solving and teamwork. Coupled with the snowballing effect as more and more of our ISP came together, it was a perfect sense of true euphoria once we were finally able to see our month of hard work pay off and mold itself into a fully functional, aesthetically pleasing video game.
